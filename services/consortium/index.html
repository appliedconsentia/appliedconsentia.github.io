Business networks

The traditional business model is a centralized organization offering multiple services. Consider the case of the car-sharing economy. In this economy, parties are transacting with each other. The traditional business model is a single organization managing transaction amongst car owners and customers seeking rides. The organization might seek to expand its offerings with added value-added services such as payment services and more, thus shifting the focus away from the transactional aspects.

This type of business model is increasingly fraught with the danger of regulatory attention on monopolistic behavior. For the parties managed by such a single organization, there is little scope for flexibility. From an investment perspective, valuing an organization with a multiple value chain is a complex affair.

A decentralized business model

In any business operation, there are transactional elements. The transactional aspects could significantly benefit from DLT based systems.

Our view is future of business models will be decentralized. In this decentralized world, we see organizations operate as independent entities fully optimized to deliver services with a singular focus on their strength.

A multi-disciplinary team comprising experts in investment, business operations, and DLT experts is needed to establish a decentralized business model.
